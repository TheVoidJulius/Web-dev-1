Ice Berg

Antarctica is home to some of the world's largest icebergs, which are formed when massive chunks of glacial ice break off from ice shelves or glaciers, a process known as calving. These icebergs, often breathtakingly beautiful, can vary significantly in size and shape, ranging from small "growlers" to colossal tabular icebergs, some measuring hundreds of kilometers in length. As fresh water ice, icebergs float in the surrounding ocean, with about 90% of their mass submerged beneath the surface. This phenomenon plays a vital role in global oceanic and climatic systems, contributing to sea level changes and influencing oceanic currents. Rainforest

The Amazon Rainforest is the world's largest tropical rainforest, spanning over 5.5 million square kilometers across eight South American countries, and it houses about 10% of the Earth's known species while playing a crucial role in global climate regulation and freshwater supply..
